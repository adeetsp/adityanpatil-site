Humans are SO emotional.

I keep getting reminded of this, and recently I heard a great quote that I loved —
it puts the whole thing in one line:

> Humans make decisions emotionally and rationalize them logically.

The decision comes first. The reasons come after. Once you hear it, you start
seeing it everywhere — in arguments, in big life choices, in the takes people
defend the hardest.

And I mean, I'm kinda guilty of it also. I've made plenty of decisions with my gut
and then assembled the logical case afterward. I'm not writing this from above
anybody.

Here's the part I can't settle, though. I still don't know if I'm convinced it was
always like this, or if I'm only like it now. Maybe humans have been emotion-first
forever and I just got old enough to notice. Or maybe something about right now is
different. I genuinely go back and forth.

Either way, the thing that gets me is this: society has come as far as it has. All
the progress, all the information, all the education — and it still just feels like
a lot for people to be *this* emotional at this point. You'd think we'd have closed
some of that gap by now.

I don't know which answer it is yet. I'm mostly just sitting with the quote.
