title: "It's the packaging, not the idea"
description: "'Growth mindset' makes me nauseous and I can't fully explain why — but I'm pretty sure it's the delivery I'm rejecting, not the idea."
category: philosophical
pubDate: 2026-07-08

Confession: any time someone says "growth mindset" I find it nauseating. I just
hate that phrase. I honestly can't even explain why. Someone can be saying
something I fully agree with, and the second those two words come out, something in
me checks out.

Maslow's hierarchy of needs gives me a version of the same thing. Belonging,
growth, self-actualization — the actual content is probably fine. But people talk
about it with this god complex, like they've unlocked the source code of being
human and you're currently on level two of their pyramid. That delivery makes me
hate a framework I'd otherwise nod along to.

And here's the thing I keep landing on: I think if the narrative was told to me a
different way, I would accept it. Same ideas, different packaging — no resistance.
Tell me "people do better when they believe they can improve" and I'm nodding.
Call it a growth mindset and I'm nauseous. Walk me through "it's hard to think
about purpose when you're worried about rent" and I'm with you. Present the pyramid
like scripture and I'm out.

So it's not the ideas I'm rejecting. It's the packaging. Which is a weird thing to
notice about yourself, because the packaging shouldn't matter — and apparently, for
me, it completely does. I can't fully explain that either.

Still not saying "growth mindset" out loud, though.
