Quick context for this one: I work in product marketing at a software security
startup, and the past year has been the steepest learning curve of my career — in the
best way. New tools, new skills, a lot of "okay I'm confused, give me the step by
step" moments, and a growing pile of things I've shipped that I'm genuinely proud of.

This bucket is where I write that stuff down. What I'm building, what worked, what
flopped, and what I'd tell myself a year ago. I'm not going to do the LinkedIn thing
where every post is a humble-brag wrapped in a lesson. Sometimes the honest update is
just "I tried a thing and it didn't land."

I'm also writing this section for a slightly selfish reason: if you're an employer,
a teammate, or someone thinking about working with me, reading a few of these will
tell you more about how I operate than any resume will.

Ambitions are big. Updates as they happen.
